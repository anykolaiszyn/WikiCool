## Purpose

This SOP defines the standard procedure for opening and operating the VCC sales booth from vehicle arrival through the first customer interaction and into the mid-shift routine. Following this checklist in order minimises setup errors, ensures compliance readiness, and keeps the booth presentable at all times.

For inventory loading and vehicle prep the night before, see [[Vehicle Inventory SOP]]. For handling age-verification edge cases, see [[Age Verification Script]].

---

## Pre-arrival (complete before leaving for the venue)

1. Confirm event address and parking/load-in instructions in the event brief.
2. Verify that the booth permit and any venue-specific vendor credential are in the document wallet — do not leave without them.
3. Check weather. If outdoor event with temperature below 60 °F or above 85 °F, add the portable humidor cooler bag to the load.
4. Confirm vehicle inventory against the packing list from [[Vehicle Inventory SOP]]. Sign and date the packing list before departure.
5. Charge the Square reader and the backup power bank. Both must read 100 % on departure.
6. Ensure the cash bank envelope is sealed, counted ($200 in small bills), and stored in the lockbox.
7. Confirm one staff member per booth station is confirmed and has the booth arrival time.

---

## Setup (arrival to 30 minutes before open)

1. Park as close to the load-in zone as permitted. Do not leave the vehicle unattended with inventory visible.
2. Unload in this order: display cases → table linens → signage → POS equipment → consumables (lighters, cutters, bags) → literature.
3. Wipe down all display surfaces with the damp microfibre cloth before placing product.
4. Arrange product by line, not by price. Face labels outward. No price tags on individual cigars — pricing is on the menu card only.
5. Set up the menu board. Confirm all current prices match the active price sheet (stored in the shared drive under `pricing/`).
6. Connect the Square reader to the phone hotspot. Run a $0.00 test transaction and confirm the receipt sends.
7. Place the "21 & over" placard at the front edge of the table, centred and visible from the aisle approach.
8. Stage the ID scanner (or manual log sheet if the scanner is unavailable) at the left side of the table.
9. Fill the humidor display and confirm hygrometer reads 68–72 % RH. If below 65 %, add a Boveda pack before opening.
10. Sign and post the permit in the visible location specified by the venue.

---

## Opening (5 minutes before start time)

1. Do a final walk-around. Check: permits posted, ID placard visible, pricing correct, humidor closed.
2. Count the cash bank again. Record the opening count on the daily cash log sheet (one per event, filed by date).
3. Confirm Square app is logged in and showing the correct event in the notes field.
4. Set all loose items (bags, cutter bowl, lighter display) so they are behind the table line — nothing should overhang into the aisle.
5. Announce "we're open" in the event staff channel if applicable.

---

## Mid-shift

1. **Every 60 minutes**: check humidor RH. If it drops below 65 %, add a Boveda pack immediately and log the time and pack number on the event sheet.
2. **After every 10 transactions**: reconcile the Square running total against the physical cash count if cash sales have been made.
3. Restock display from the reserve box under the table as units sell. Do not leave empty slots visible.
4. If a product runs out, pull its menu card slot insert and replace with the "Sold Out" card.
5. Report any customer incident (dispute, medical, loss prevention) to the venue floor manager and record in the incident log on the shared drive within 30 minutes.
6. Do not leave the booth unstaffed for more than 5 minutes. If both staff need to step away simultaneously, close and lock the display case and drape the table linen over the setup.

---

## Close

1. Count cash and record the closing total on the daily cash log. Separate the $200 bank float into the return envelope.
2. Run the Square end-of-day summary report and screenshot it to the event photos album.
3. Pack product in reverse load order: literature → consumables → POS equipment → signage → table linens → display cases.
4. Cross-check packed inventory against the packing list. Record any discrepancies (damage, units sold, missing items) in the event notes.
5. Seal the cash deposit envelope. Do not leave it in the vehicle overnight — deposit at the bank or secure in the office safe the same evening.
6. Return venue credential and any borrowed equipment to the event organiser before leaving.
7. Submit the completed event sheet to the shared drive under `events/YYYY-MM-DD-event-name/` by end of day.
